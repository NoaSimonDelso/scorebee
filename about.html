About the project

For many, the decision-making process in Brussels remains obscure – yet these decisions affect the lives of 500 million citizens across Europe.

This tool makes it easier for citizens to find out know how politicians voted on decisions that affect their future – on climate change, shale gas and genetically modified crops. It will help them find decide who they want to vote for, and to hold those they choose accountable for their promises.

This website analyses voting records of Members of the European Parliament (MEPs). Sandbag Climate Campaign focused on policy crucial to prevent devastating climate change. Friends of the Earth Europe concentrated on policy to stop the expansion of genetically modified crops and shale gas development in Europe. The project aims to improve transparency and to help European citizens hold their MEPs accountable. It is neither endorsement nor condemnation of individual MEPs or their political parties and groups.

Organisations

Friends of the Earth Europe is the largest grassroots environmental network in Europe, uniting more than 30 national organisations with thousands of local groups. We are the European arm of Friends of the Earth International which unites 74 national member organisations, some 5,000 local activist groups, and over two million supporters around the world. Our campaigns focus on today's most urgent environmental and social issues. We challenge the current model of economic and corporate globalization, and promote solutions that will help to create environmentally sustainable and socially just societies. We seek to increase public participation and democratic decision-making.

Find out more at www.foeeurope.org

Sandbag Climate Campaign is the NGO leading in data-focused campaigning for effective emissions trading. Through expert analysis, hard-hitting reports and targeted advocacy we aim to shine a light on what’s working and what’s not on the carbon markets. By campaigning for reform in the EU Emissions Trading Scheme, and in carbon markets from California to Beijing, we can prevent the emission of billions of tonnes of greenhouse gases.

Find out more at: www.sandbag.org.uk
